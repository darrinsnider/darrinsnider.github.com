INTECHRATE

trend analysis

A quick look into how and why we use our many devices throughout our day-to-day lives, and — more importantly — an examination of how these interactions are affecting our desires and popular concepts of the role technology will play in the future.

It used to be scary, but we have grown to LOVE technology ... when it plays nicely. Items that until recently were viewed as accessories that only the youngest generation could operate or even benefit from are now so commonplace we are attaching them to our wrists. Intechrate, or the long-term trend of integrating this technology, leaves us constantly thirsting for more — as seen in the following subtrends.

We treat these digital brains as human companions more and more everyday. We talk to them, ask them for opinions, and trust them with our biggest secrets. Naturally, we look for this warm, familiar affection in return.

"Your phone can do WHAT?" Every new feature is one too few. We have a perception of technology that leads most to believe absolutely anything and everything is possible — and it comes in a package that fits in your average pocket.

Sometimes a personal device can't do it all alone. With the Internet of Things, every product has the opportunity to come to the digital dark side, in turn allowing us to design right in our environments and create interactions that bridge the gap between digital and physical.

Wearable tech has created a culture of effortlessly monitoring and recording our personal health — in great detail. This opens many opportunities to benefit healthcare and well-being, but how much oversight is too much?

Controlling our devices can be monotonous and time-consuming, so we look for them to take note of our habits and perform tasks on their own. The perfect balance of automatic and manual control hinges on one thing: convenience.
